Our Story

Our humble beginnings started way back in the 1930s as Fred’s Cafe. A decade later the ownership changed and it became Zim’s Cafe in the 1940s. In 1960, yet again, the ownership changed and the cafe became the Coffee Mill. Twenty five years later, Debbie started waitressing at the Coffee Mill in 1985. At that time her son, Eric, was two years old. Only four years later, the Coffee Mill closed and the owners took everything with them.

Debbie, however, was determined to become the new owner, even though she had to literally start from scratch. Over the next year, with a lot of help and support from loyal cafe customers (who had expertise in various trades), she was able to reopen it as the Encinitas Cafe in 1989!

Two years later she enlisted her dad, Herb, to help out with the wait list on busy weekends. Long time cafe customers will forever remember him as the taller elderly gentleman who could keep the hungry at bay, while also making them laugh with his good sense of humor.

In the fall of 2024, Debbie formally retired and passed the baton to her son, Eric. He was basically raised in the cafe and grew up learning all the ins and outs of the business! Eric’s wife, Erica, has also been a mainstay of the cafe as a beloved waitress for over 15 years.

The Encinitas Cafe is very fortunate to have a dedicated staff. It is full of long term employees, who work together as a team, and look out for each other like family. As everyone knows, there’s a lot to be said for a restaurant that was not only able to survive, but is now thriving despite all of the setbacks during the pandemic.

When you come to the cafe you’ll notice our homey feel and be greeted with a smile. We love to both take care of our regulars and welcome newcomers with the same fast and friendly service. We hope to serve you soon!
